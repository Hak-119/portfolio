A Deep Learning-Based Approach to Intelligent Web Vulnerability Discovery and Risk Assessment

ETAACT 2026 — IEEE Xplore

The fast growth of modern web applications has made them more vulnerable to complex and new types of vulnerabilities, which in turn has exposed the drawbacks of traditional scanners that perform static crawling and depend on signature-based detection. To go beyond the capacity of traditional scanning workflows, we introduce a learner-driven system that not only detects web vulnerabilities but also analyzes the risks brought by them. The core of our solution is a combination of the Open Web Application Security Project Zed Attack Proxy (OWASP ZAP) and a Deep Q-Network (DQN) crawler that dynamically identifies security-critical application paths, a Light Gradient-Boosting Machine (LightGBM) classifier that focuses on prioritizing risks from the point of view of exploitability, and a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM)-based autoencoder that identifies anomalous Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) patterns. Results from experimentation indicate that our method is significantly better than a simple signature-based scanning method in terms of reducing false positives, improving prioritization accuracy and extending scan coverage. An analytical look into Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) shows how they can be used in creating adaptive payloads and finding zero-day vulnerabilities. The findings indicate that the use of deep learning (DL), ensemble learning, and reinforcement learning (RL) together leads to a major increase in the capability levels and effectiveness of contemporary web vulnerability assessment systems.
